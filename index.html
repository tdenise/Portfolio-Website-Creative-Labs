Hi, I'm Denise Tran

About Me

Denise Tran is a second-year student studying computer science at California State Polytechnic University, Pomona. She is passionate about design, tech, and innovation. At the moment she is the social chair and science council representative for sheCodes, a club that empowers women in STEM. Her interest in computer science stemmed from attending her first hackathon in high school which led her to realize her goal which is to find her niche within Computer Science and to expand upon it.

PROJECTS

HearMe

Took part in the development of an iOS application which connects users in need of consultation to police officers, legal professionals, and therapists anonymously during ChimeHack 3, a hackathon with a focus on creating solutions for girls and women through tech.s

BOA HealthBand

Part of a team that developed a wristband and accompanying beta Android application that monitors blood pressure 24 hours a day, 7 days of the week through the use of Arduino hardware.

Banana Piano

During sheCodes’ annual Future Women Developers Conference I introduced computer science to high school girls through a guided Raspberry Pi project as a mentor. By the end of the conference, each student made a working banana piano using a few alligator clips, lots of bananas, a Raspberry Pi, and the magic of python!
